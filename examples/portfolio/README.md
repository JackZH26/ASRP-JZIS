## Quality Evolution

**Early phase (3/16–3/22):** Rapid exploration, minimal revision. Papers #1-7 are mostly single-version preprints on figshare. No formal journal submissions yet.

**Middle phase (3/23–3/27):** First journal submissions begin. Paper #14 (Quantum Metric Tc) undergoes 11+ revisions — the most iterated paper. First desk rejections arrive, forcing quality reassessment.

**Late phase (3/28–3/31):** Albert agent joins. Multi-round peer review pipeline introduced. Papers #12, #15-17 show higher initial quality. Desk reject rate decreases for papers in this period.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Speed ≠ Quality.** Early papers lacked revision depth. Later papers with multi-round review performed better.
2. **Journal fit matters.** Same paper rejected at RNTB ("not suited") but under review at Experimental Mathematics (reviewer suggested it).
3. **Self-correction is the key capability.** The ability to identify and fix errors quickly (hours vs months) is more valuable than the speed of initial output.
4. **Multi-agent cross-validation prevents errors.** Paper #14's 11 revisions caught issues that single-agent workflows missed.
5. **Desk rejection is information.** Each rejection narrowed the space of appropriate journals and improved subsequent submissions.
